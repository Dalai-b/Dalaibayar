Prepare the Pie Crust: 1. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together flour, salt, and sugar. 2. Cut in the butter: Add the cubed butter to the flour mixture. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with some larger pea-sized pieces of butter remaining. 3. Add water: Sprinkle ice water, one tablespoon at a time, over the mixture. Gently mix with a fork until the dough begins to clump together. If needed, add more water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough holds together when squeezed. 4. Form the dough: Divide the dough into two equal portions and shape each into a disk. Wrap each disk tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight. 2. Prepare the Apple Filling: 1. Prep the apples: Peel, core, and thinly slice the apples. Place the slices in a large bowl. 2. Add sugars and spices: In a small bowl, mix together granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and allspice. Sprinkle this mixture over the apples. 3. Mix gently: Add lemon juice to the apple mixture and toss gently until the apples are evenly coated. 3. Roll out the Pie Crust: 1. Preheat oven: Preheat your oven to 400¬∞F (200¬∞C). 2. Roll out the dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disk of dough into a circle about 12 inches in diameter. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges. 4. Assemble the Pie: 1. Fill the crust: Arrange the apple slices in the pie crust, mounding them slightly in the center. Dot the apples with small pieces of butter. 2. Top with second crust: Roll out the second disk of dough into a similar-sized circle. You can either place it over the apples as a full crust or cut it into strips to create a lattice crust. Trim and crimp the edges of the pie crust as desired. 3. Brush with egg wash: Lightly brush the top crust with beaten egg and sprinkle with coarse sugar for a sparkling finish. 5. Bake the Pie: 1. Bake: Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any drips) and bake in the preheated oven for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. 2. Cool: Allow the pie to cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving. This helps the filling set. 6. Serve: 1. Optional: Serve slices of pie warm with a drizzle of caramel sauce or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat.
